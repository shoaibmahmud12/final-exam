Salmon

Origin: The Atlantic Salmon, scientifically known as Salmo salar, is native to the Atlantic Ocean. This species of salmon is primarily found in rivers along the North Atlantic, encompassing regions such as North America, Europe, and Scandinavia. Atlantic Salmon is renowned for its distinctive flavor and is a popular choice among seafood enthusiasts.

Price: 500 gram = 15$

Preparation Recommendations: 

Grilled Salmon:

            Marinate salmon fillets in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, and your favorite herbs (such as dill or thyme).
            Grill the salmon on a preheated grill for about 4-5 minutes per side or until the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
            Serve with a squeeze of fresh lemon and a side of grilled vegetables.
            Baked Lemon Garlic Salmon:
            
            Place salmon fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
            Drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt, pepper, minced garlic, and fresh lemon slices.
            Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 12-15 minutes or until the salmon is cooked through.
            Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.
            Pan-Seared Salmon:
            
            Season salmon fillets with salt, pepper, and a pinch of paprika.
            Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with olive oil.
            Sear the salmon fillets skin-side down first for about 3-4 minutes, then flip and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until golden brown and cooked through.
            Serve with a squeeze of lemon.
            Salmon Ceviche:
            
            Cut salmon into small, bite-sized pieces.
            Marinate the salmon in a mixture of lime juice, diced red onion, chopped cilantro, diced tomatoes, and a pinch of salt.
            Let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.
            Serve the ceviche with tortilla chips or on a bed of mixed greens.
            Salmon Teriyaki:
            
            Make a teriyaki sauce using soy sauce, brown sugar, minced garlic, and ginger.
            Marinate salmon in the teriyaki sauce for at least 30 minutes.
            Grill or broil the salmon until it's caramelized and cooked to your liking.
            Serve over rice and drizzle with additional teriyaki sauce.
            Poached Salmon with Dill Sauce:
            
            Poach salmon fillets in a mixture of white wine, water, lemon slices, and fresh dill.
            While the salmon is poaching, prepare a dill sauce using mayonnaise, Greek yogurt, chopped fresh dill, and lemon juice.
            Serve the poached salmon with the dill sauce.

Shrimp

Origin: 

Whiteleg Shrimp (Litopenaeus vannamei):

            Origin: Originally native to the eastern Pacific Ocean, particularly the Pacific coast of Central and South America. However, due to aquaculture, whiteleg shrimp are now farmed globally, including in Asia, the Americas, and other regions.
            Black Tiger Shrimp (Penaeus monodon):
            
            Origin: Native to the Indo-Pacific region, including the Indian Ocean and the western Pacific Ocean. Black Tiger Shrimp are found in coastal areas from East Africa to Southeast Asia.
            Brown Shrimp (Crangon crangon):
            
            Origin: Native to the northeastern Atlantic Ocean and the North Sea. Brown shrimp are commonly found along the coasts of Europe, from the Mediterranean Sea to the northern areas of the Atlantic.
            Northern Shrimp (Pandalus borealis):
            
            Origin: Found in the cold waters of the northern Atlantic and northern Pacific Oceans. Northern shrimp are distributed along the coasts of North America, Europe, and Asia.
            Pink Shrimp (Farfantepenaeus duorarum):
            
            Origin: Primarily found in the western Atlantic Ocean, including the Gulf of Mexico and the southeastern United States. Pink shrimp inhabit coastal areas and estuaries.
            Spot Prawn (Pandalus platyceros):
            
            Origin: Native to the northeastern Pacific Ocean, particularly along the coasts of North America from Alaska to California.

Price: 20$ per pounds

Preparation Recommendations: 

Garlic Butter Shrimp:

            In a skillet, melt butter and sauté minced garlic until fragrant.
            Add peeled and deveined shrimp to the skillet and cook until they turn pink.
            Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
            Garnish with fresh parsley and serve over pasta or rice.
            Grilled Shrimp Skewers:
            
            Marinate shrimp in a mixture of olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, and your favorite herbs (such as parsley or cilantro).
            Thread shrimp onto skewers and grill for 2-3 minutes per side or until cooked through.
            Serve with a dipping sauce made from yogurt and chopped dill.
            Shrimp Scampi:
            
            Cook linguine or spaghetti according to package instructions.
            In a pan, sauté shrimp in olive oil, garlic, and red pepper flakes until they turn pink.
            Add white wine, lemon juice, and chicken broth. Simmer until the sauce slightly thickens.
            Toss the cooked pasta in the shrimp and sauce mixture. Garnish with chopped parsley.

Sustainability Practices

Responsible Fishing Practices: Partnering with local fishermen who adhere to sustainable and responsible fishing methods, avoiding practices that harm marine ecosystems or deplete fish populations.

            Traceability: Implementing rigorous traceability systems to track the journey of our seafood from ocean to plate. This transparency allows our customers to make informed choices and know the origin of the products they purchase.
            
            Conservation Efforts: Actively supporting marine conservation initiatives aimed at protecting endangered species, preserving habitats, and promoting biodiversity in our oceans.
            
            Reducing Environmental Impact: Implementing eco-friendly practices throughout our supply chain to minimize waste, energy consumption, and carbon footprint. This includes exploring packaging alternatives and waste reduction strategies.
            
            Community Engagement: Collaborating with local communities to foster sustainable fishing practices, promote fair labor conditions, and contribute to the well-being of the regions from which we source our seafood.
            
            Educational Initiatives: Educating our customers about the importance of sustainable seafood choices, providing information on responsible consumption, and sharing insights into the positive impact of their decisions on the environment.
